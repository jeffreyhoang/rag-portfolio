# About Me

## Who I Am

I am a 22-year-old computer science graduate student originally from
Sacramento, California, currently based in San Jose while pursuing my
Master of Science in Artificial Intelligence at San Jose State University. I
am resourceful, ambitious, and detail-oriented by nature — someone who
approaches problems strategically and follows through with precision.
I am equally comfortable working independently and collaborating
within a team, and I thrive in environments that challenge me to grow
and push beyond what I already know.

---

## Why Computer Science and AI

I chose computer science because I believe technology — and
artificial intelligence in particular — is one of the most powerful
tools available for improving people's lives at scale. AI is not just
a growing field, it is a transformational one, and I want to be
someone who builds within it rather than observes from the outside.
What drives me most is the idea that the systems I build could one
day reach people I will never meet and make something in their life
a little easier, a little better, or a little more possible. That
sense of impact is what gets me excited to sit down and work.

---

## What I Do

My primary interest is in machine learning engineering — building
models and data-driven systems that go beyond experimentation and
actually function in production. I enjoy the full arc of that process:
understanding the data, designing the model, evaluating its behavior,
and deploying something that works reliably in the real world. I am
particularly drawn to applied AI at the intersection of machine
learning and software engineering, where the challenge is not just
getting a model to perform but getting it to perform consistently
inside a larger system.

My technical background spans deep learning, computer vision, and
full-stack AI application development, built through research
experience at Cal Poly Pomona's Computational Intelligence Lab and
personal projects including a production-deployed RAG assistant that
powers this very portfolio.

---

## How I Work

I approach every problem with a combination of strategic thinking and
genuine curiosity. I do not just want to complete a task — I want to
understand it deeply enough to do it well and identify ways to do it
better. I am organized and methodical in how I work, but I stay
flexible when the situation calls for it, which it often does in
fast-moving technical environments.

One of the most formative experiences in shaping how I communicate
and collaborate has been coaching youth basketball. Working with
young athletes taught me that the best ideas mean nothing if you
cannot articulate them in a way the other person can actually receive.
I learned to put myself in someone else's shoes before I speak —
to understand their frame of reference first and then communicate
in a way that genuinely lands. I bring that same approach to
technical collaboration: whether I am explaining a model architecture
to a non-technical stakeholder or giving feedback to a teammate,
I try to make sure the message does not just leave my mouth but
actually crosses their mind.

## Outside of Work

When I am not building or studying I am either in the gym boxing,
on the court playing basketball, or coaching youth athletes with the
Silicon Valley Soldiers. I genuinely enjoy being around people and
building real connections — not surface-level networking, but the
kind of relationships where you actually know and look out for each
other. That instinct to connect and invest in others carries into
how I work with teammates and mentors in professional settings as well.

## What I Am Looking For

I am looking for a role as a machine learning engineer or AI software
engineer at a company where I can contribute meaningfully from day
one while continuing to grow. I am open to any industry within the
tech world that intersects with AI and software engineering — what
matters most to me is the quality of the work, the challenge of the
problems, and the people I get to learn from. Company size is not a
factor; I am equally comfortable in a fast-moving startup and a
structured larger organization. What I am looking for is a team that
values craft, takes its work seriously, and welcomes someone who is
ambitious, hungry to learn, and ready to be pushed.

I am based in California and prefer to stay in the state, though I
am open to relocation for the right opportunity. I am also open to
remote and hybrid arrangements. I am available to begin immediately
as long as the schedule accommodates my ongoing graduate coursework
at San Jose State University.
