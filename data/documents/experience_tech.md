## Student Research Assistant — Computational Intelligence Lab
*August 2024 – December 2025 | Department of Computer Science, Cal Poly Pomona*
*Supervisor: Dr. Hao Ji, Ph.D., Associate Professor*

Research engineering role focused on computer vision, human pose
estimation, and deep learning infrastructure within an academic
high-performance computing environment.

Developed web-based automation tools and REST APIs to control
multi-camera GoPro setups using Python, the OpenGoPro API, and USB,
BLE, and Wi-Fi communication protocols, significantly increasing data
collection efficiency and reducing manual overhead for multi-view
recording sessions. Trained deep neural networks for 3D keypoint
detection on the Human3.6M dataset using TensorFlow on NVIDIA A100
GPUs across high-performance computing clusters, applying batch
normalization, ReLU activation, dropout, and linear layer architectures
to improve model accuracy and generalization across unseen poses.

Developed a physics-based pipeline that fitted Metrabs 3D keypoint
predictions to biomechanical LocoMuJoCo models, applying forward
kinematics and optimization techniques to estimate joint angles and
body scaling parameters. The result was anatomically consistent and
physically accurate 3D human pose alignments suitable for downstream
biomechanics analysis and simulation.

Technologies: Python, TensorFlow, OpenGoPro API, NVIDIA A100, HPC
clusters, forward kinematics, MuJoCo, Human3.6M dataset.
